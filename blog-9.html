Law Freshmen Guide: 7 Entry-Level Legal Internships to Jumpstart Your Career

Law Freshmen • Recruitment

Introduction: Stepping into law school can feel overwhelming, especially when older students start talking about corporate applications. But you don’t need to land a magic circle scheme on day one to build a great resume.

If you are a first- or second-year law student looking to gain early exposure, here are 7 entry-level legal internships that are perfectly suited for beginners. 👇

01 | Local & Boutique Law Firm Internships 💼

The Scope: Small-to-medium-sized local law firms are generally much more receptive to hiring Year 1 to Year 3 students.

How to Apply: Most firms outline their application process on their website (usually a simple email with your CV and cover letter), or you can track active openings on general job portals.

Note: International and Magic Circle firms have highly stringent academic requirements and complex application cycles that primarily target penultimate-year students. Local firms are your best bet for early experience!

02 | Mini Pupillage (Chambers Internship) ⚖️

The Scope: A mini pupillage involves shadow-learning under a practicing Barrister, typically for an unpaid 2-week period. You will shadow them in courtrooms and spend down-time at their Chambers.

The Reality: Because mentors know your legal knowledge is still developing, you won't be managing major files. Common tasks include reading trial bundles, conducting basic legal research, proofreading, and chatting over lunch.

Pro-Tip: If you are dead-set on the Bar, a mini-p can become highly intensive. Many students extend their stay for several weeks to tackle complex structural research assignments.

03 | Court Marshalling (Shadowing a Judge) 🏛️

The Scope: Similar in principle to a mini pupillage, court marshalling is a structured form of job shadowing where you apply directly to a Judge via email.

The Experience: You essentially shadow a judge throughout their daily routine, observing court proceedings from an elite insider perspective and learning how judicial decisions are formulated.

04 | Government Legal Internships 🏛️

The Scope: These placements are usually advertised via university mass emails. After filling out an application, you must be nominated by your law faculty to secure an interview or offer.

The Perks: These are well-structured, paid internships (typically offering a monthly allowance of HK$10,000+) lasting 2 to 3 months. They offer invaluable exposure to the inner workings of government legal departments (like the Department of Justice).

05 | In-House Corporate Legal Internships 🏢

The Scope: Most multinational corporations and major local enterprises house their own internal legal departments. Larger corporate teams frequently hire legal interns to assist with contract management and compliance.

How to Apply: Keep an active eye on mainstream job boards, Google Jobs, and LinkedIn using keywords like "Legal Intern" or "In-house Legal Undergraduate."

06 | Legal Tech Start-up Internships 💻

The Scope: Legal Tech start-ups are highly active recruiters for university students. Because their operations span beyond traditional law, they do not require advanced legal knowledge.

The Experience: You can step into non-traditional legal tracks such as digital marketing, legal content creation, or product development while experiencing a fast-paced start-up culture.

07 | Academic Research Assistant (RA) 📚

The Scope: Law faculties frequently source undergraduate RAs via mass university emails or internal career portals.

How to Stand Out: If you achieved top grades in a specific module or built a good rapport with a professor, your chances of being hired are exceptionally high. Working as an RA deepens your understanding of specific legal concepts and looks stellar on academic resumes.

A Quick Reality Check from Me:

Please do not let this list spark unnecessary hustle anxiety! My honest advice is to enjoy your first year of university to the absolute fullest. Go to orientation camps, experience hall life, join student societies, and make memories.

I didn't start applying for internships until my second year, and everything turned out completely fine. Take it one step at a time! ⚖️

Ending Note:

Early internships are simply about discovering what you like (and what you don't like) in the legal industry.
