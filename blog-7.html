From Vacation Scheme to Training Contract: How to Stand Out and Convert Your Internship

Vacation Scheme • Conversions

Introduction: Securing a Vacation Scheme (Vac Scheme) is an incredible achievement, but the real challenge is converting that short internship into a full-time Training Contract (TC). To stand out among a cohort of equally ambitious interns, you need to master three core pillars: Attitude, Work Quality, and Cultural Fit.

In Big Law, missing even one of these can cost you the offer. Here is the ultimate blueprint on how to impress your supervisors and secure your return offer. 👇

01 | Attitude: The Easiest Value to Demonstrate 💡

Execution Over Entlement

Showing that you genuinely care about the work and the firm is the most straightforward yet necessary requirement. Hard work, a willingness to learn, and humility are non-negotiable.

  • Be Responsive & Reliable: The most direct ways to show your commitment are being willing to pull overtime hours (after all, commercial law is inherently a long-hours profession) and replying to emails quickly. Responsiveness requires zero technical skill, yet it is highly expected.
  • Stop Being a "Spoon-Fed" Intern: Take your tasks seriously. Never ask a question that could be answered by a quick Google search or an internal database look-up. When you get stuck, always spend time trying to find a potential solution before approaching a senior associate.
02 | Work Quality: Meticulous Execution and Thoughtfulness 🧠

Flawless Deliverables for Premium Rates

Law firms charge premium rates because their work is flawless. Your output must reflect that level of professionalism, especially during your assessment weeks.

  • Obsess Over Attention to Detail: The legal profession demands extreme accuracy. Even a minor typo or a formatting mistake can leave a negative impression on partners who value precision. Proofread everything twice.
  • Leverage Your Hard Skills: Different practice areas require different skill sets. For instance, Litigation teams heavily prize sharp legal research skills, while teams handling cross-border mainland Chinese clients will put a premium on your professional Chinese language proficiency.
  • Practice Empathy and Thoughtfulness: Put yourself in the shoes of the senior associate reviewing your work. Communicate clearly and structure your research memos so they are incredibly easy to read, digest, and cross-check.
03 | Cultural Fit: The Unteachable Element 🤝

Cracking the Firm Core DNA Alignment

This is the one factor you cannot fake or prepare for. Every international law firm has its own distinct personality DNA. Some firms favor extroverted, highly proactive go-getters; others prefer quiet, deeply focused hard-workers.

Don't Take Rejection Personally: Once you spend enough time in the industry, you realize that firm culture is a very real thing, and colleagues within the same firm often share similar traits. If you do not secure a return offer, do not view it as a personal failure. It usually just means the cultural puzzle pieces didn't click, and there is a platform out there that is a far better match for your personality.

Insider Note: Understanding Selection Mentalities ⚖ings

Different firms approach the vacation scheme conversion process with entirely different hiring mindsets:

  • The Elimination Mindset (Large Intake Firms): Firms that recruit a large batch of trainees annually (e.g., 10+ trainees) often use the vacation scheme to filter out candidates who are a poor fit. Because the standard of interns entering the door is already exceptionally high, if you perform consistently, work hard, and avoid major red flags, you have a very strong chance of being retained.
  • The Perfectionist Mindset (Boutique / Small Intake Firms): Firms that only hire 1 to 2 trainees a year operate under a strict "quality over quantity" rule. To land an offer here, your performance must be absolutely stellar and completely flawless across every single department.

Ending Note:

No matter which type of firm you find yourself in, control what you can control. Put your best foot forward, stay authentic, and let the rest fall into place. Trust that the right firm will recognize your value!
