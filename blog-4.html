5 Things My Training Contract Gave Me: What I Gained Beyond My Law License

Personal Reflections • Realities of Practice

Introduction: Securing a Training Contract (TC) is the ultimate goal for almost every law student. But once you actually cross that finish line and start your seats, you realize the transformation goes way deeper than just getting qualified.

Reflecting on my TC journey at a top-tier firm, here are the 5 unexpected things I walked away with—beyond the law license.👇

01 | A Sense of Identity

Finding an Anchor in My 20s

The most immediate shift after starting my TC was a newfound sense of professional identity. When people ask what I do, I can finally answer with calm, quiet confidence. Being a "law student" or "grad" always felt slightly floating and unstable. In the natural chaos and uncertainty of your mid-20s, this career handed me a solid anchor, making me feel like I truly belonged to a respected, professional community.

02 | Financial Autonomy

The Confidence of My First "Real" Income

Working at a US law firm means being on the Cravath Scale. Earning a starting salary of $60,000+ HKD a month straight out of university changed my relationship with money. It wasn’t just about upgrading my lifestyle—though being able to order whatever I want at TamJai, blast the A/C without guilt, or grab daily coffees is great. It was about security. It allowed me to build a financial cushion much faster than most peers, giving me the peace of mind that I could survive months of unemployment if needed. More importantly, it allowed me to give back—contributing to my parents' household and fully sponsoring a family vacation. That feeling is irreplaceable.

03 | Resilience

Evolving Through Modern Corporate Warfare

Pulling consecutive all-nighters, taking a cab home at 3:00 AM, and getting back up at 8:00 AM is a suffocating cycle. Add to that the corporate reality of apologizing for mistakes that weren’t yours, or handling clients complaining about things out of your control. Let's be clear: I refuse to romanticize this toxic hustle as "essential growth." Most of that suffering is unnecessary. But what I am grateful for is the version of myself that refused to break. The version that, despite being running on empty, stood firm and got the job done. My TC taught me that I am far tougher than I ever gave myself credit for. That raw resilience is now my greatest weapon.

04 | Gratefulness for the Mundane

Reclaiming "Dead Time"

Before Big Law, going for a walk by the beach, hitting the gym, or taking a midday nap felt completely ordinary. Now, I realize that "uninterrupted time" is the ultimate luxury. Today, if I can just turn off my phone notifications for an hour to go on a hike, I feel incredibly rich. This TC journey taught me a harsh but beautiful lesson: never take ordinary freedom for granted. Even if I eventually pivot away from Private Practice, I will carry this profound respect for slow living with me.

05 | Radical Focus

Breaking Free from Digital Doom-Scrolling

You might find this hard to believe, but my personal phone screen time averages just 2 hours a day. While many people my age are caught in the trap of doom-scrolling and comparison-induced anxiety on social media, I am forced to hyper-focus on complex legal analysis and execution. Yes, I've sacrificed leisure time. But I’ve also managed to bypass a massive amount of digital noise and superficial FOMO. If we are destined to burn our youth on something intensive, I'd much rather spend it sharpening a highly specialized skill set that no one can take away from me, rather than envying someone else's curated life.

Ending Note:

To anyone currently in the trenches of their TC or stressing over apps: it’s going to test you in ways you didn't know possible. But the version of you that comes out on the other side will be unstoppable.
