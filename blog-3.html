Revealing The 4 Unwritten Rules of Coffee Chats

Networking • Vacation Scheme Tips

Introduction: If you are anything like I was when I first entered the corporate legal world, the concept of a "Coffee Chat" probably leaves you a bit confused. Whether it happens during an internship or a casual networking event, learning how to handle these chats is crucial.

My very first coffee chat happened during a Vacation Scheme. I saw my fellow interns actively inviting Partners out for coffee, so I jumped on the bandwagon out of FOMO—without having any clue what to expect or what to say! Today, we are breaking down the unwritten rules of law firm coffee chats so you don’t have to wing it. 👇

01 | Timing

Freedom with a Side of Personal Responsibility ⏳

Unless a lawyer is underwater with meetings or racing against an urgent deadline, scheduling a coffee chat during regular office hours is perfectly acceptable.

The Big Law Trust Policy: Most international law firms (i-firms) don't require you to be glued to your desk all day. They treat their employees as responsible, self-disciplined adults. Taking a 30 to 60-minute break for a quick coffee or even an ice cream run is generally fine, provided your billable work gets done.

The Punctuality Rule: Because coffee chats are casual, seniors might push your meeting back by 10 or 15 minutes due to sudden client emergencies. That is completely normal. However, as an intern or trainee, you must never be the one who is late.

02 | Content

Balancing Small Talk with Real Value 🗣️

While the setting is relaxed, you are still there to talk about your legal career. Do not expect to spend the entire hour talking about your hobbies or weekend plans.

Steer Toward Strategy: Starting with light small talk to build a rapport is an excellent social move, but the core of the chat should revolve around professional topics.

The Ultimate Icebreaker: Always prepare a concise answer to: "How are you finding the vacation scheme so far?" Almost every partner or associate will use this to kick off the conversation. Don't get caught off guard!

03 | Purpose

Go in with an Agenda 🎯

In the corporate world, a coffee chat generally serves two main purposes: (a) gathering insider information, or (b) seeking genuine professional advice.

The 3-Question Rule: If you initiate a coffee chat with someone at the firm during your internship, you must prepare at least 3 thoughtful questions. These can focus on their specific practice area, typical working hours, or firm culture—anything you are genuinely curious about.

Be Specific: Remember, the person sitting across from you is likely very busy. They will highly appreciate highly specific, targeted questions rather than vague ones like "What is it like to be a lawyer?"

04 | Mindset

Curiosity Over Performance 💡

Approach the conversation with a desire to learn, not a desire to "perform." Some students treat coffee chats like a stealth interview and launch into a heavily rehearsed elevator pitch about themselves. This almost always leaves an artificial, negative impression.

People Love to Share: Most professionals enjoy talking about their own journeys. You will make the senior feel valued by being genuinely inquisitive about their career milestone (e.g., "How did you end up specializing in this field?" or "What was your biggest challenge as a junior associate?").

Avoid the "Interrogation" Trap: Do not just fire off question after question without contributing to the dialogue. Share your own thoughts and reflections on what they say to ensure a natural, two-way conversation. As a rule of thumb, aim to speak about 30% of the time and listen for the remaining 70%.

Ending Note:

Mastering the casual coffee chat is one of the best ways to stand out during a vacation scheme and secure that elusive Training Contract return offer. Stay curious, stay professional, and let your personality shine through!
